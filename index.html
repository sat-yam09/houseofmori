OUR PHILOSOPHY

A SANCTUARY OF STONE AND FOREST

The name "Mori" means forest in Japanese. HŌM is a physical manifestation of a dialogue between the woodland architectures of Japan and the historic materials of India.

Built in Silvassa, our sanctuary features sweeping butterfly clay roofs, soaring exposed-brick arches, and screens fashioned from locally sourced bamboo. The design marries the spatial restraint and respect for nature of Zen minimalism with the deep, enveloping warmth and luxurious textures of Rajput architecture.

Here, light falls through slatted bamboo in geometric patterns onto hand-pressed earthen tiles. The air smells of rain on clay, freshly ground cardamom, and toasted matcha.

Our culinary ethos mirrors this dialogue—incorporating high-precision Japanese techniques with the heritage-rich spices and recipes of Western India. Every plate is curated, intentional, and served with Rajput hospitality (Mehmaan Nawaazi) in an atmosphere of unhurried presence.

DAYTIME AT HŌM

CAFÉ HŌM

By day, the light through our butterfly roof filters onto the hand-painted tiles of the café counter.

Café HŌM is a slow-paced daytime sanctuary. It serves as a quiet meeting ground for writers, artists, and creators. We feature an exclusive selection of single-estate Indian coffees sourced from the Western Ghats and ceremonial-grade Uji matcha whisked by hand. Our confectioneries are a visual tribute to Japanese wagashi aesthetics, cooked with traditional Indian confectionery techniques.
